TRAINING MODULE 4

Training Focus: Speed, power hitting, shot selection.
Training Modules:
   Speed Work:Improve running between the wickets and reaction time.
   Power Hitting Training: Develop the ability to clear the boundary.
   Shot Repertoire:Expand range of shots to find scoring opportunities.
Speed Work:
  Interval Sprints:
    Warm-up: 10 minutes of jogging followed by dynamic stretches.
    Main Workout: 8-10 intervals of 100-meter sprints at maximum effort, with 2 minutes of rest between each interval.
    Cool Down: 5-10 minutes of light jogging followed by static stretching for major muscle groups.
  Agility Ladder Drills:
    Set up an agility ladder on the ground.
    Perform drills such as high knees, lateral shuffles, and two-feet-in each square jumps.
    Complete 2-3 sets of each drill for 30 seconds, with 1 minute of rest between sets.
    Focus on quick footwork and maintaining proper form throughout each drill.
  Reaction Ball Drills:
    Stand facing a wall with a reaction ball (a ball that bounces unpredictably).
    As the ball rebounds, react quickly to catch or hit it back against the wall.
    Perform 3 sets of 30-second drills, aiming to react as quickly as possible to each bounce.
    Rest for 1-2 minutes between sets.
Power Hitting Training:
  Medicine Ball Slams:
    Hold a medicine ball above your head with both hands.
    Explosively slam the ball onto the ground as hard as possible.
    Aim for 3 sets of 10-12 repetitions, resting 1-2 minutes between sets.
  Batting with Resistance Bands:
    Attach resistance bands to a sturdy object and loop them around your waist and bat handle.
    Practice your batting technique while feeling resistance from the bands.
    Perform 3 sets of 15-20 batting strokes, focusing on driving through the ball with power.
  Box Jumps:
    Stand in front of a sturdy box or platform.
    Explosively jump onto the box using both feet.
    Step down and repeat for 3 sets of 8-10 repetitions, resting 1-2 minutes between sets.
Shot Repertoire:
  Net Session with Variations:
    Practice different types of shots such as drives, cuts, pulls, sweeps, and lofted shots.
    Rotate through various drills focusing on specific shot types, spending 5-10 minutes on each.
    Experiment with different angles, heights, and placements to develop a diverse shot repertoire.
  Scenario-Based Training:
    Set up scenarios such as chasing a target, setting a target, or batting against specific types of bowlers (fast, spin).
    Practice adapting your shot selection and technique to match the match situation.
    Focus on improvisation and decision-making under pressure.
  Visualization and Mental Rehearsal:
    Spend time visualizing yourself playing different shots in various match scenarios.
    Mentally rehearse the execution of shots, focusing on technique, timing, and placement.
    Use visualization techniques to reinforce positive shot selection and build confidence in your shot repertoire.

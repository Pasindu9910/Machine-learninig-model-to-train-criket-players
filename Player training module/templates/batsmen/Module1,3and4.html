MODULE 1,3 AND 4

Training Focus:Core strength, upper body strength, lower body strength, endurance, Batting technique, consistency, mental preparation, Speed, power hitting, shot selection
Training Modules:
  Core Strength Exercises:Enhance stability and balance for prolonged batting stints.
  Upper Body and Lower Body Strength Exercises: Aid in generating power for boundary-hitting shots and running between the wickets.
  Endurance Training:Build stamina for batting long innings and converting starts into substantial scores.
  Batting Technique:Refine shot execution and shot selection for consistent run-scoring.
  Mental Preparation:Develop concentration and focus techniques to maintain consistency.
  Video Analysis:Study own batting footage to identify and rectify technical flaws.
  Speed Work:Improve running between the wickets and reaction time.
  Power Hitting Training: Develop the ability to clear the boundary.
  Shot Repertoire:Expand range of shots to find scoring opportunities.

Core Strength:
  Plank Circuit:
    Perform 3 sets of planks for 30 seconds each, with 1-minute rest between sets.
    Include side planks and plank variations (e.g., plank leg lifts) for a balanced core workout.
Upper Body Strength:
  Push-up Pyramid:
    Perform sets of push-ups starting with 5 reps, then increasing by 1 rep each set until failure.
    Rest for 30 seconds between sets.
Lower Body Strength:
  Squat Jumps:
    Perform 3 sets of 10 squat jumps, focusing on explosive power.
    Rest for 1 minute between sets.
Endurance:
  Interval Runs:
    Run at a moderate pace for 2 minutes, followed by a 1-minute sprint.
    Repeat for 20 minutes.
Mental Preparation:
  Visualization Exercise:
    Spend 10 minutes visualizing successful batting scenarios, focusing on positive outcomes and confident mindset.
Batting Technique:
  Throwdowns:
    Have a coach or partner deliver throwdowns, focusing on specific batting techniques (e.g., forward defense, cover drives).
    Practice each technique for 10 minutes.
Speed:
  Sprint Intervals:
    Sprint at maximum effort for 30 seconds, followed by 1 minute of rest.
    Repeat for 10 intervals.
Power Hitting:
  Medicine Ball Slams:
    Perform 3 sets of 12 medicine ball slams, focusing on explosive power.
    Rest for 1 minute between sets.
Shot Repertoire:
  Net Session with Variations:
    Practice different types of shots such as drives, cuts, pulls, sweeps, and lofted shots.
    Rotate through various drills focusing on specific shot types, spending 5-10 minutes on each.
    Experiment with different angles, heights, and placements to develop a diverse shot repertoire.
  Scenario-Based Training:
    Set up scenarios such as chasing a target, setting a target, or batting against specific types of bowlers (fast, spin).
    Practice adapting your shot selection and technique to match the match situation.
    Focus on improvisation and decision-making under pressure.
  Visualization and Mental Rehearsal:
    Spend time visualizing yourself playing different shots in various match scenarios.
    Mentally rehearse the execution of shots, focusing on technique, timing, and placement.
    Use visualization techniques to reinforce positive shot selection and build confidence in your shot repertoire.
